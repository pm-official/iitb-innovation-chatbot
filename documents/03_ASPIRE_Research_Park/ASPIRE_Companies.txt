Applied Materials India & IIT Bombay
A Strategic Two-Decade Collaboration
Since its inception in 2005 with a few sponsored programs, Applied Materials India’s partnership with IIT Bombay has evolved into a model of strategic industry-academia collaboration. A major milestone came in 2015, when Applied became the first anchor client at IIT Bombay’s Research Park—enabling seamless collaboration with faculty and students, and direct access to advanced labs and infrastructure.
Over the past 20 years, this partnership has grown into a nationally recognised initiative—featured as a case study in Kotler’s Essentials of Modern Marketing (2024)—and continues to set benchmarks in collaborative innovation.
Key Milestones & Impact
India’s First University-Based 200 mm Fab Facility: Enabling hands-on research and development in semiconductor technologies.
Three On-Site Labs: Focused on nanotechnology, chemistry, and material sciences.
Semiconductor Certification Program: A pioneering initiative to build a skilled workforce in India.
Industry Affiliate Member of SEMIX: Driving ecosystem development through training, research, and student engagement.
Over 100 Sponsored Research Projects: Advancing cutting-edge technologies across diverse domains.
National Research Centers: Co-established the Center of Excellence in Nanoelectronics (CEN) and the National Center for Photovoltaics Research & Education (NCPRE).
Exploration Centre: A Hub for Co-Innovation
Applied Materials’ dedicated Exploration Centre at IIT Bombay is equipped with advanced R&D tools and serves as a launchpad for taking innovations from lab to fab. It is a vibrant, innovative space for students and faculty to collaborate on real-world challenges and accelerate scientific breakthroughs.
Shaping the semiconductor ecosystem
Applied Materials India remains committed to expanding this collaboration into emerging areas such as nanofabrication, sustainable materials, chemical sciences, health sciences, and advanced manufacturing. Applied Materials’ sustained engagement with IIT Bombay exemplifies its unwavering commitment to advancing India’s semiconductor ecosystem and fostering long-term innovation.
Applied Materials India
Associated with IITB since 2005
In line with its commitment to IIT Bombay and in support of India’s vision of becoming an electronics manufacturing hub, Applied Materials India became the first anchor client at IIT Bombay’s Research Park in 2015. The facility enables close interaction with faculty and student researchers and benefits from seamless access to labs, equipment and services. The journey that started with few sponsored programs in 2005, over the last decade and half has grown exponentially to include India’s first university –based facility with 200 mm fab tools, three on-site labs focusing on Nanotechnology, Chemistry & Material Sciences and R&D on next-generation technologies,
and a jointly offered Semiconductor Technology and
Manufacturing Certification Program, the first of its kind in India.
The collaboration has also catalyzed the establishment of two national research centers at IIT Bombay
– the Center of Excellence in Nanoelectronics (CEN) and the National Center for Photovoltaics Research & Education (NCPRE). Applied Materials India has sponsored more than 100 projects as a research partner for IIT Bombay to develop cutting-edge technology in various areas. As Applied Materials India looks to the future with IIT Bombay as an ‘institute of eminence’, it finds an opportunity to expand this visionary collaboration into more exciting and emerging areas like nanofabrication, manufacturing and materials, sustainable chemical science, health sciences and engineering.

CORE
Engineering Innovation, together: CORE x IIT Bombay at CETRA
CORE Energy Systems Ltd., founded over two decades ago, stands at the forefront of India’s engineering landscape as a trusted EPC and advanced manufacturing partner. With proven expertise in nuclear, defence, aerospace, oil &amp; gas, and clean energy, we are driven by a singular purpose: to deliver mission-critical solutions that combine precision, innovation, and sustainability.
The launch of CETRA (Core Energy Technology and Research Analysis) at IIT Bombay’s Research Park, Aspire marks a defining step in our journey. This collaboration with IIT Bombay unites academic excellence with industrial depth to address the evolving engineering challenges of our time. CETRA is envisioned as a vibrant hub where researchers, engineers, and the scientific community co-create advanced solutions, from reactor safety systems and defence technologies to sustainable energy frameworks.
Beyond research, CETRA is about impact: translating pioneering ideas into scalable technologies that strengthen India’s self-reliance while contributing to global progress. It is where the rigour of academia meets the pragmatism of industry, forging innovations that endure.
Together, CORE and ASPIRE not just to meet today’s needs but to shape the future of critical engineering – for India, and the world.

SustLabs
Making Homes Energy Smart
SustLabs, incubated at SINE, IIT Bombay in 2019, has been on a mission to make every home more energy-efficient, safe, and sustainable. Guided by the visionary incubation philosophy of SINE — backing high-risk, deep-technology companies — SustLabs has grown from its early prototypes to deploying its solutions with over 10,000 households across 100+ cities. The mentorship and guidance from industry stalwarts, friends in the campus, professors and each and everyone have been instrumental in shaping this journey.
With a strong foundation in R&D, SustLabs has published research papers, filed patents, and consistently pioneered industry-first solutions. From launching the first-generation Ohm Assistant in 2021 to today’s fourth-generation technology, SustLabs now enables real-time electricity monitoring, appliance-level insights, and even advanced fire detection — providing alerts up to 20 minutes before a potential electrical fire.
Our collaborations span marquee real estate developers like Rustomjee, Piramal, Prestige, and Agami, as well as global OEM partners including Schneider Electric, Panasonic, and Inepro (Netherlands). SustLabs is still very young and very hungry to grow, and create technologies that shape healthier, safer, and more sustainable cities.

InSolare
Associated with IITB Research Park since June 2024
InSolare Energy has steadily evolved from a leading solar EPC player into a forward-looking clean energy innovator with a strong focus on green hydrogen and indigenous electrolyser technology. Building on a solid foundation in renewable project execution, the company has taken bold strides into deep-tech R&D.
A key initiative currently underway is the setup of a dedicated cell and stack development & test facility at IIT Bombay, aimed at benchmarking the performance of InSolare’s in-house electrolyser stack against global standards. This collaboration marks a significant step toward strengthening India’s self-reliance in critical clean energy technologies.
The company’s R&D team, based at IIT Bombay, has been instrumental in developing our patented electrolyser stack, compatible with both PEM and AEM electrolysis technology, with potential reversible fuel cell applications.
InSolare’s ambitious roadmap includes establishment of 250–300 MW electrolyser manufacturing facility in India, as well as allocations under the MNRE SIGHT scheme – 10 MW electrolyser capacity and 19,000 MT of green hydrogen production, positioning it among the key players in India’s green hydrogen ecosystem.
Through a blend of innovation, collaboration, and execution excellence, InSolare is driving the transition to a cleaner, self-reliant energy future.
