Our Labs

IIT Bombay

IIT Bombay’s Tinkerers’ Laboratory is a student-driven space, managed solely by students under Prof. Ramesh Singh’s guidance. Inaugurated in 2014, it facilitates practical learning through over 400 projects and 1800+ course projects. The lab supports innovation with the ‘Makerspace 101’ curriculum and fosters entrepreneurship, evidenced by its contribution to 10 startups. It was also the first Tinkerers Lab to be inaugurated

IIT Hyderabad

Located at IIT Hyderabad, this 1000 sq. ft. Tinkerers Lab, supports projects focused on innovation, and technology. The lab’s Flagship event THRUST, the de-jure TechFest of IIT Hyderabad, and has secured sponsorships from companies like Bosch, Mathworks, Ansys, Silicon Labs, Hexagon, Suzuki Innovation Center, and Airforce.

IIT Gandhinagar

Located at IIT Gandhinagar, the Tinkerers’ Lab fosters a strong tinkering cultural facility Providing students access to tools like laser cutters and 3D printers, the lab empowers them to realise their ideas. With 462 unique users and 3764 hours of usage in 2022-23, its impact is significant. Beyond local activities, the lab strengthens the broader makerspace network by hosting Tinkeract, an All India Tinkerers’ Lab Meet.

Pravara Rural Engineering College, Loni

The Tinkerers’ Lab at Pravara Rural Engineering College (PREC Loni) operates under Guidance of Prof Suhas Kale. This lab supports student projects, including an IOT project designed to help farmers with soil constituent data and the development of a robot for Dussehra celebrations. They also serve as learning hub by organising workshops and learning session on the 3D printers, Laser cutter and Aurdino Bootcamps for other local institutes and schools

IIT Jammu

Established in 2021, the IIT Jammu Tinkerers’ Lab is a 1800 sq. ft. facility that provides 24-hour access to students and faculty, fostering a space for innovation and hands-on learning. The lab aims to cultivate innovation skills, promote indigenous production, and develop futuristic skills like complex problem-solving. In the 2023-24 period, the lab supported 414 projects, with the 3D printer being the most popular machine. The lab has shown significant growth in events and outreach, and IIT Jammu is planning to integrate it into a larger Institute Innovation & Entrepreneurship Development Center to further enhance its capabilities and impact.

Nirma University

The Tinkerers’ Lab at Nirma University, Ahmedabad, is a platform for students to achieve recognition for their engineering projects. An example is the development of an Agastya 4.1(Remotely Operated Underwater Vehicle) that won 1st prize in Aligarh Muslim University Remotely Operated Vehicle Challenge 3.0 National Level competition. The lab’s activities are also showcased through media like a shared ‘Octa Pod’ video.

BITS Pilani, Pilani Campus

The Tinkerers’ Lab at BITS Pilani is a 750 sq. ft. student-run makerspace established in 2022. The lab has played a crucial role in supporting various BITS Pilani projects and technical teams. Key projects include the development of a rover by CRISS and the Cansat project by Team Anant. The lab has assisted technical teams in streamlining operations and manufacturing components for competitions, such as rover wheels, CanSat fins, and pivot steering mechanisms.

ICT Mumbai

With its recent launch at ICT Mumbai in May 2023, this chemical Tinkerers’ Lab is one of its kind where students develop problem-solving skills by introducing tinkering as a path to advanced engineering and manufacturing practices. This lab is managed under the guidance of faculty Prof. Vishwanath Dalvi.

IIT Bombay Chem E

This ChemE Tinkerers’ Lab by the Department of Chemical Engineering at IIT Bombay was inaugurated in 2022 to facilitate “hands-on” learning for students while equipping them with a physical environment that is conducive to technological innovations. This lab is managed under the guidance of faculty Prof. Venkat Gundabala.

IIT Indore

IIT Indore’s Tinkerers’ Lab, inaugurated on September 11, 2023, is intended to foster a culture of making and innovation within the institute. Typically, Tinkerers’ Labs function as makerspaces equipped with tools like 3D printers, laser cutters, and other equipment that enable students to design and build prototypes. It is probable that the IIT Indore lab supports academic projects, personal explorations, and participation in competitions, providing a collaborative environment for students from various disciplines

IIT Ropar

Dr. Ranbir Singh Tinkerers’ Lab acts as a creative hub for aspiring engineers and makers to tinker, build, and innovate. Equipped with state-of-the-art tools and technology. Open access beyond academic hours to allow students to turn their ideas into reality.

Prince Shri Venkateshwara Padmavathy Engineering College

Spanning 2200 sq. ft., the Tinkering Lab at PSVPEC is a dedicated innovation space designed to promote hands-on learning, creativity, and interdisciplinary collaboration. Equipped with tools for embedded IoT, robotics, EVs, and 3D modelling, the lab empowers students to turn ideas into real-world solutions. Managed by student teams and faculty, it enables advanced projects and prototype development, fostering a strong culture of innovation and entrepreneurship on campus.

Ahmedabad University

The Tinkerers’ Lab at Ahmedabad University is a student-managed, 24×7 innovation space designed to support hands-on learning, creative exploration, and real-world problem-solving. Equipped with tools and materials for electronics, fabrication, and design, the lab complements existing facilities, such as the Prayas Shala and Fabrication Shop. It enables students to work on academic projects, co-curricular activities, startup ideas, and competitions, all under the guidance of a supervisor. The lab fosters interdisciplinary collaboration and nurtures a culture of technical innovation on campus.

College of Engineering Roorkee University

Located in Roorkee, Uttarakhand, the Tinkering Lab at COER is a dynamic innovation space within the Incubation Center, spanning 2025.76 sq. ft. It is equipped with advanced tools such as 3D printers, CNC machines, and electronics workbenches. Designed to foster creativity, support research, and encourage entrepreneurship, the lab provides students and scholars with the resources to transform ideas into functional prototypes and real-world solutions.

Teerthanker Mahaveer University, Moradabad

Located in Moradabad, Uttar Pradesh, the Tinkerers’ Lab at TMU serves as a dynamic space for experimentation, innovation, and project-based learning. Outfitted with essential tools for electronics, mechanical prototyping, and digital fabrication, the lab enables students to transform their ideas into functional prototypes. It supports academic coursework, personal innovations, and interdisciplinary team projects, fostering a strong maker culture on campus.

National Institute of Engineering

ALPHA (Advanced Laboratory for Programming, Hardware, and Analytics) Innovation and Tinkerers’ Laboratory is Karnataka’s first twin-lab model combining High-Performance Computing and Electromechanical Prototyping. The two labs were inaugurated on May 21st 2025, in partnership with Lenovo and Motorola. Situated adjacent to Mysuru’s industrial belt, ALPHA lab is poised to host joint R&D projects, internships, and knowledge-transfer initiatives with local manufacturers.

Chitkara University

Located in Village Jhansla, Punjab. Chitkara University is a leading institution situated in the Chandigarh region. Known for its focus on innovation, research, and hands-on learning, the university houses the Tinkerers’ Lab, established in 2025 under the guidance of Prof. Vijay Bhadon. With collaborations like the Cobotics Education Enhancement Initiative with MBF and Addverb Technologies Limited, the university continues to provide cutting-edge resources such as state-of-the-art collaborative robotic arms (cobots) to enhance education and research.

IIT Jodhpur

Situated in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, the Anand Rathi Tinkerers’ Lab (ARTL) at IIT Jodhpur is a state-of-the-art, 3040 sq. ft. student-run makerspace established in 2025. Supported by Anand Rathi Financial Services Limited in collaboration with the Maker Bhavan Foundation, the lab serves as a creative hub for innovation, design thinking, and hands-on experimentation. Equipped with modern fabrication tools and guided by the Professors-in-Charge, Dr. Bhivraj Suthar and Dr. Rajit Ranjan, ARTL aims to nurture a culture of problem-solving and interdisciplinary collaboration among students, empowering the next generation of innovators, entrepreneurs, and technologists at IIT Jodhpur.

Sanjivani University

Located in Maharashtra, Sanjivani University is committed to advancing practical education and research through immersive learning environments. The university houses a 1,600 sq. ft. Tinkerers’ Lab, established to encourage innovation, problem-solving, and real-world application of engineering principles. The lab is supervised by Prof. Dr. Kiran Wakchaure and Prof. Dr. Tanay Ghosh, who mentor students in building impactful solutions through hands-on experimentation and collaborative projects.

Navrachna University

Situated in Vadodara, Gujarat, Navrachana University is a forward-looking institution with a strong emphasis on innovation, experiential learning, and industry integration. The university houses a 1,200 sq. ft. Tinkerers’ Lab, aimed at enabling hands-on learning, prototyping, and applied research. Guided by Prof. Ravi Sevak, the lab is equipped with state-of-the-art collaborative robotic arms (cobots), empowering students to work on advanced automation, robotics, and interdisciplinary engineering projects aligned with real-world industry applications.
